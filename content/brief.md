---
title: "NAB 2026 floor day one: IP infrastructure matures, AI enters live production"
date: "2026-04-19T16:00:00Z"
---

The exhibit hall opened Sunday with hardware density and AI integration dominating the announcement slate. **Evertz** debuted the X-CALIBER 1RU encoding platform with up to 128 JPEG XS paths, while **Apex-RF** launched RAINIER, a Wi-Fi 7 wireless camera system delivering 4K HDR with native ST 2110 output. **AJA Video Systems** introduced BRIDGE LIVE IP, a hardware appliance for bi-directional encode and decode between uncompressed SMPTE ST 2110 and compressed streaming codecs — a practical solution for facilities running mixed infrastructure. **AVer Information** unveiled the TR615, a 4K/60fps AI-tracking PTZ camera with NDI HX3 bundled at no extra charge, targeting IP-native live production and event workflows.

On the software side, **Witbe** is showing an AI-native monitoring platform that replaces script-based test automation with natural language commands across real devices and live networks — a meaningful shift for streaming QA teams that have relied on engineer-maintained script libraries. **Telestream** formally launched Pulse for ST 2110 monitoring alongside its UP cloud orchestration service. The Sports Summit opened its expanded four-day run with NBC Sports' Jon Miller on rights strategy, now accessible to all standard floor pass holders.

The broader show numbers frame the floor activity: NAB reports AI-focused exhibitors are up 82% year-over-year, and creator attendance is expected to more than double — a structural shift that NAB EVP Karen Chupka frames as convergence rather than expansion. Sports Video Group's North Hall preview confirms ST 2110 has moved from transition story to operational baseline, with real-time AI augmentation now the competitive frontier in live sports production.

The **NAB Streaming Summit** opens Monday in the West Hall with sessions on sports streaming economics, AI chatbot discovery data, and a BritBox fireside featuring BBC Studios DTC CEO Robert Schildhouse. Watch whether the efficiency-over-expansion theme visible in floor hardware translates into streaming business model sessions.
