title: "NAB 2026 peak floor day: AI adoption data, sports rights debate, and new production platforms"
date: "2026-04-21T23:00:00Z"
---

The third full day on the exhibit hall floor confirmed two trends running through NAB 2026: AI has moved from adjacent topic to structural show theme, and live sports rights strategy is generating more debate than any single product launch. TV Technology's survey of the show floor found 238 AI-focused exhibitors — an 82% year-on-year increase — and two AI pavilions in West Hall where there was one in 2025. **Microsoft** VP Silvia Candiani put it plainly: "AI is moving from experimentation to real, scaled adoption." The exhibitor count backs that up.

On the production and infrastructure floor, **Grass Valley** showcased Framelight X, a federated platform that eliminates file transfers between capture, ingest, editing, and publishing by treating the entire workflow as one continuous pipeline. The announcement is distinct from Grass Valley's AMPP Alliance ecosystem highlighted earlier in the week. **Bolin Technology** launched three hardware products — the Range all-weather IP65-rated PTZ, the R9-L420N broadcast camera with Genlock and LTC timecode, and the KBD Plus controller with a four-channel IP preview display. **intoPIX** used its 20th anniversary to demonstrate JPEG XS SMPTE 2110 workflows running on standard off-the-shelf PCs via a new Titanium SDK, removing the hardware barrier that has slowed IP production adoption.

At the Sports Summit, **Dolby OptiView** introduced an Ad Engine with hybrid SSAI/SGAI insertion and a Sports Intelligence personalization layer, with IBM and AWS on the same panel. The Streaming Summit's niche sports session put contrasting business models on record: World Racing Group charges $300/year for 80–90 races while **ESL FACEIT Group** makes all esports content free, with co-streaming influencers accounting for up to 50% of event viewership. NBC Sports President **Jon Miller** argued directly that broadcast television remains the strongest live sports rights platform and that Peacock is designed to complement, not replace, NBC's linear signal.

Wednesday is the final day, with the exhibit hall closing at 2pm PT. Watch for ATSC 3.0 infrastructure deal announcements, post-show attendance figures from NAB organizers, and any remaining Streaming Summit data releases from Dan Rayburn on pay TV versus streaming viewing hours.
