---
title: "Pre-show day: vendor platforms, summit lineups, and AI moving to production"
date: "2026-04-18T19:45:00Z"
---

NAB Show 2026 opens its pre-show conferences in Las Vegas today with the exhibit hall set to open Sunday, April 19. Attendance has risen to 18,000-plus represented companies — up from 12,000 in 2025 — and two dedicated AI Innovation Pavilions are making their debut. Analyst **Devoncroft Partners** and commentator Carlo De Marchis warned pre-show that generic AI pitches will fall flat; the wave of vendor announcements today suggests exhibitors have absorbed that signal.

On the tools and infrastructure front, **Synamedia** claims its updated Quortex PowerVu can reduce US affiliate distribution costs by up to 80% by shifting final assembly of linear services to the CDN edge. **Imagine Communications** marks ten years of SMPTE ST 2110 advocacy with the compact SNP-XS processor and Linux-based XVR playout engines on subscription pricing. **Grass Valley** is demonstrating its AMPP Alliance ecosystem with over a dozen integrated partners spanning camera robotics, cloud automation, and IP connectivity. **LiveU** is anchoring its IP-video portfolio with the new LU900Q, a 5G field unit with native eSIM, MIMO antenna, and 4:2:2 encoding. **Chyron** turns 60 with updates to PRIME, PAINT, and CAMIO — including NFL Next Gen Stats integration and AI image tagging in beta. **Microsoft** is signalling a more visible presence at the show, with Simon Crownshaw positioning Azure AI Copilot as a deployable newsroom tool today, not a roadmap item.

The summit tracks are shaping up as substantive. The Sports Summit adds **FanDuel** as the first sportsbook operator on the stage, alongside Religion of Sports CEO Ameeth Sankaran and San Francisco 49ers executive Laura Johnson. The Streaming Summit (April 20–21) dedicates a full session to live-test data on AI chatbots as content discovery interfaces — the first time the Summit has treated AI as a front-end user experience question rather than a back-end workflow problem. JioStar CTO Akash Saxena's session on orchestrating 72 million concurrent viewers remains the most technically demanding slot on the agenda.

What to watch when the floor opens Sunday: whether **Grass Valley's** open-ecosystem AMPP strategy holds up against proprietary rivals, how Synamedia's 80% cost-reduction claim lands with affiliate engineers, and whether Microsoft's stepped-up presence translates into visible co-demonstrations with Avid, Vizrt, and Ross Video partners on the floor.
