title: "NAB floor day one: AI authenticity, cloud production deals, and sports summit live"
date: "2026-04-19T15:00:00Z"
---

The exhibit hall opened Sunday with vendor announcements spanning content authenticity, cloud production partnerships, and AI-generated social distribution. **Sony** drew attention by confirming the PXW-Z300 XDCAM as the first video camera to implement the C2PA content provenance standard, embedding cryptographically signed authenticity metadata at the point of capture — a direct hardware response to growing concern over AI-generated footage in news and sports workflows. On the cloud production side, **TVU Networks** and **Tencent Cloud** announced a joint platform combining TVU's microservices architecture with Tencent's container infrastructure, citing the Paris Games Torch Relay and 369 simultaneous BBC election streams as prior deployments.

**Quickplay** used the first floor day to launch Social Signals, an AI tool that converts trending moments into social-ready vertical clips in minutes, while simultaneously announcing that Gray Media — managing 163 websites, 326 mobile apps, and 815 CTV apps — has consolidated onto a single Quickplay deployment. Television New Zealand completed a parallel 12-month migration to Quickplay on AWS. **Insta360** previewed the Luna Series, two cameras co-engineered with Leica featuring 1-inch sensors and 10-bit color, marking the company's push into conventional professional camera territory alongside wireless microphone and stabilizer hardware. **Triveni Digital** announced the StreamScope MT-70, an MPEG analyzer built specifically for Brazilian broadcasters managing the simultaneous ISDB-Tb and TV 3.0 simulcast transition.

The Sports Summit launched its expanded four-day run with a market-overview panel featuring **Genius Sports**, the Utah Jazz, and the Oklahoma City Thunder — all floor pass holders can attend for the first time this year. International Cricket Council CEO **Sanjog Gupta** is appearing on the Summit stage, bringing context from JioHotStar's 72 million concurrent viewer peak during the ICC T20 World Cup to an audience of broadcast and streaming engineers.

The NAB **Streaming Summit** opens Monday in the West Hall with BBC Studios DTC CEO Robert Schildhouse delivering the opening keynote on BritBox's North America strategy — the first time a BBC Studios executive has headlined the Summit opener. Watch whether cricket's distribution scale and the Quickplay/Gray Media consolidation signal a broader platform-rationalization trend across the week's remaining sessions.
