title: "Pre-show day: AI from codec layer to live keying, NextGen TV goes global"
date: "2026-04-18T15:30:00Z"

NAB Show 2026 opens its pre-show conferences today in Las Vegas with the exhibit hall doors set for Sunday, April 19. Attendance has jumped to 18,000-plus represented companies — up from 12,000 in 2025 — and AI exhibitors have nearly doubled year-over-year, with two new AI Innovation Pavilions making their debut. **Devoncroft Partners** and analyst **Carlo De Marchis** warned pre-show that generic AI pitches will fall flat; the vendor announcements landing today suggest exhibitors have absorbed the message.

On the tools side, **Adobe** is entering public beta with Color Mode in Premiere, mounts Frame.io cloud projects as local storage via Frame.io Drive, and adds Kling 3.0 models to Firefly Video — three distinct workflow gaps addressed in a single pre-show drop. **Vizrt** is arriving with the AI Keyer, which separates talent from backgrounds in real time without green screens or specialist lighting, targeting news, sports, and enterprise production from any location. **Telos Alliance** is debuting the Omnia XII FM/HD/DAB processor with a new Clemenza FM clipper co-designed by founder Frank Foti, shipping July 2026. **Avid** extends its earlier AWS announcement with a joint Google Cloud demonstration of Gemini-powered agentic workflows inside Media Composer — natural-language search, B-roll suggestion, and autonomous multi-step orchestration running at both companies' NAB booths.

The conference programme frames two strong summit storylines for the week. The **Streaming Summit** (April 20–21) will surface the PGA TOUR's operational AI pipeline — 7,000 short-form highlight clips generated weekly — alongside a live sports infrastructure track featuring Amazon Prime Video, DAZN, Warner Bros. Discovery, and NASCAR. On the broadcast policy side, a featured April 20 session brings Brazil's Communications Minister and FCC Commissioner Olivia Trusty together on the **NextGen TV** / ATSC 3.0 global transition, with Pearl TV simultaneously launching its converter box program to close the household adoption gap.

What to watch when the floor opens Sunday: whether agentic AI demonstrations at Avid and Google Cloud hold up under press scrutiny, how the ATSC 3.0 hard-cutoff debate lands with US broadcasters, and whether **V-Nova's** pre-show argument — that human-vision codecs are structurally wrong for machine-vision AI workflows — surfaces in any vendor product conversations on the show floor.
