---
title: "NAB exhibit hall opens with IP integration, AI video services, and cloud scale on day one"
date: "2026-04-19T15:00:00Z"
---

The NAB 2026 exhibit hall opened Sunday for its first floor day, and the announcements that hit during the morning and afternoon sessions underline three converging priorities: software-defined production pipelines absorbing dedicated hardware, AI moving from demonstration to deployed service, and cloud multiview proving commercial scale. **Calrec** and **Grass Valley** formalized a software integration that puts Calrec's ImPulseV virtual DSP audio inside the AMPP cloud-native platform — a single operator surface covering audio and video without dedicated DSP hardware. **Panasonic** and NEP Group announced certified interoperability between the KAIROS IT/IP switcher and NEP Platform, built on SMPTE ST 2110 and NMOS, letting operators manage switching and resource allocation from one unified interface.

On the AI services front, **AWS** introduced Elemental Inference — a cloud service that automates vertical video reformatting, clip event detection, and personalized video generation. Fox Sports, Grabyo, and ScorePlay are all presenting use cases at the AWS Theater. **Ross Video** added a HighField AI integration for automated news graphics creation alongside the milestone of the Ultrix routing frame passing 5,000 deployments across 120 countries.

In live sports streaming, **MediaKind** disclosed that its MK.IO Multiview platform now reaches over 20 million subscribers in North America, with Charter using it for the 2026 NCAA basketball tournaments and Comcast offering viewer-assembled NFL game layouts to X1 subscribers — all without bespoke hardware at the operator edge. On the hardware side, **Canon** brought the CINE-SERVO 40-1200mm cinema servo lens — extendable to 1,800mm — to the floor at $79,999 shipping September 2026, while **Atomos** debuted the Sumo PRO-19 monitor-recorder-switcher at $2,899 and disclosed its acquisition of reference-monitor specialist Flanders Scientific.

Watch for whether cloud-native master control services — including the Encompass and Techex "Altitude Events" platform announced this week — gain traction with regional sports and live-events broadcasters through the next three floor days, and whether the Streaming Summit sessions Monday and Tuesday sharpen the cost-per-subscriber calculus for operators weighing these cloud services against on-premises infrastructure.
