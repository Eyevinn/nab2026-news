title: "NAB floor day one: density, IP audio, AI newsrooms, and a sector revenue dip"
date: "2026-04-19T16:15:00Z"
---

The first full floor day at NAB 2026 brought a wave of hardware and platform announcements targeting operational efficiency in flat-budget environments. **Evertz** debuted the X-CALIBER, a 1RU encoding platform packing up to 128 JPEG XS paths or 64 HEVC/H.264 paths with hot-swappable blades — a density milestone that directly addresses the rack-space constraints of large-channel live sports facilities. **Apex-RF** launched RAINIER, a Wi-Fi 7 wireless camera system delivering 4K HDR at under 50ms latency with native SMPTE ST 2110 output and multi-vendor intercom, available now for productions moving to IP-native workflows.

**Telestream** formally introduced Pulse, a software-defined test and measurement platform for ST 2110 environments, alongside its UP cloud orchestration service. Both are now embedded inside Grass Valley's AMPP ecosystem, giving AMPP operators end-to-end signal observability without a separate monitoring stack. **Sennheiser** opened the Spectera wireless platform to third-party integration via a new OpenAPI layer and unveiled two AMBEO 2-channel spatial audio renderers — one for VOD post-production, one for real-time live encoding — while confirming the end of LinkDesk development in favour of SoundBase. **FOR-A** shipped the HVS-Q12 2 M/E switcher immediately and added AI-assisted node-graph generation to its IMPULSE GPU production platform.

Off the floor, the numbers framing the week came from **Devoncroft Partners**, whose 2025 Big Broadcast Survey — drawing several thousand respondents across 100-plus countries — found media technology sector aggregate revenue fell 2.4% in 2025. That figure is reshaping vendor conversations from capability roadmaps toward efficiency arguments. A TVNewsCheck analysis adds that agentic AI is crossing from demo into deployment in newsrooms, with Moments Lab, Mimir, Tedial, Cuez, and Avid all presenting production-ready tools at the show.

The Streaming Summit opens Monday in the West Hall, with sessions on sports bundling economics, Warner Bros. Discovery CTO Avi Saxena on global DTC engineering, and live sports streaming market data from conference chair Dan Rayburn. Watch whether the efficiency-over-expansion theme that runs through the floor hardware announcements carries into the streaming business model discussions.
