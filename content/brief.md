title: "Pre-show day: AI agents, 100G cameras, and tomorrow's Sports Summit set the tone"
date: "2026-04-18T16:00:00Z"
---

NAB Show 2026 opens today in Las Vegas with conferences and workshops — the exhibit hall doesn't throw its doors open until Sunday, April 19. That hasn't stopped the news flow. Pre-show embargoes are lifting and the 2026 thesis is already visible: **Devoncroft Partners** and **Carlo De Marchis** are telling exhibitors that generic "AI strategy" slides will land flat this year. Buyers want production-grade, governed, cost-modelled deployments. **Blue Lucy** is making that tangible with a talk on AI-governance workflows for broadcasters.

On the product side, the cameras are making the loudest pre-show noise. **Blackmagic** has unveiled the URSA Cine Immersive 100G for Apple Immersive Video. **Sony** is lining up its R Series alongside the new XYN spatial-capture workflow and MOXELA — all slated for the West Hall when doors open tomorrow. **RED's** Cine-Broadcast Module is already shipping and cited as running live on CBS and NBC Sports. **Avid** is using its keynote slot to put Content Core on AWS with Amazon MGM Studios as the launch customer, and **Harmonic** has detailed Spectrum X Plus with its own AI Orchestration layer targeting cost-per-asset pressure in playout.

The two summits dominate the programme from Sunday. The **Streaming Summit** (April 20–21) lines up leaders from Paramount, Prime Video, NBCUniversal, and JioStar — whose CTO will deep-dive on the 72-million-concurrent T20 World Cup stream. The four-day **Sports Summit**, anchored by NBC Sports' Jon Miller and powered by Lumen, runs April 19–22 with sports-rights, betting, and fan-engagement conversations that have never had this much stage time at NAB.

What to watch when the floor opens Sunday: cloud-native post production, ATSC 3.0 updates, and the first vendor reactions to how loudly the "show us, don't tell us" message lands.
