---
title: "Fox Sports, Dolby, and ATSC 3.0 deals headline a full second floor day"
date: "2026-04-20T23:55:00Z"
---

Peak floor day at NAB 2026 produced a dense cycle of IP production deals, broadcast audio firsts, and industry data points that collectively reinforce the show's central thread: the infrastructure of television is being rebuilt from satellite and hardware toward cloud, IP, and managed services. **Fox Sports** made the day's most operationally concrete announcement, confirming it is standardizing on [Appear's X Platform](https://www.tvtechnology.com/production/live-production/fox-sports-taps-appear-x-platform-for-remote-production) across its REMI infrastructure, a 104-match 2026 soccer event, and its premium Jewel Events workflow — a single-vendor IP transport commitment spanning every tier of a major rights holder's production stack.

On the broadcast infrastructure side, **TV Azteca** and **Dolby Laboratories** announced the first delivery of [Dolby Atmos immersive audio over ATSC 1.0](https://www.tvtechnology.com/platform/broadcast/tv-azteca-to-bring-dolby-atmos-to-free-to-air-tv-in-mexico) in Mexico, with Imagine Communications and Ateme as partners — putting spatial sound on free-to-air antennas without requiring receiver upgrades. **Castanet Corporation** demonstrated a [commercial ATSC 3.0 and 5G Broadcast Internet hybrid network](https://www.tvtechnology.com/platform/broadcast/castanet-launches-hybrid-atsc-3-0-and-5g-broadcast-internet-pilot-network-in-vagas) covering roughly 60% of the US population via LPTV spectrum, with founder Vern Fotheringham declaring "the broadcast internet era is no longer theoretical." **AccuWeather** completed a full migration of its broadcast operations to [Amagi's cloud-based managed Master Control Room](https://www.tvtechnology.com/infrastructure/accuweather-taps-amagi-managed-services-to-upgrade-broadcasting-operations), consolidating linear and streaming into one platform.

The industry-organization story of the day came from the IABM, which [rebranded as IAMT](https://www.tvtechnology.com/business/iabm-rebrands-as-the-international-association-of-mediatech) — International Association of MediaTech — and launched MAI, an agentic AI discovery platform built on its accumulated knowledge base. The rebrand signals how far the membership's center of gravity has shifted from dedicated broadcast hardware toward software and cloud platforms. Meanwhile, [Viaccess-Orca data](https://www.viaccess-orca.com/blog/streaming-in-the-americas-2026) circulating at the show put streaming at 47.5% of all US TV viewing in December 2025, with ad-supported tiers now representing 46% of SVOD subscriptions — numbers that framed nearly every monetization conversation on the floor.

Watch Tuesday's Streaming Summit for **Warner Bros. Discovery** CTO Avi Saxena on global DTC platform engineering, and the Sports Summit for continued rights and distribution strategy sessions as the show moves into its final peak day before Wednesday's early close.
