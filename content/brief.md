---
title: "Streaming Summit closes with AI infrastructure sessions as NAB floor heads into final day"
date: "2026-04-21T16:30:00Z"
---

Tuesday at NAB 2026 belonged to the Streaming Summit's second and final day, with the most operationally detailed sessions of the two-day program. **Warner Bros. Discovery** CTO Avi Saxena outlined the engineering demands of running **Max** as a genuinely global DTC platform — localization, latency, and AI infrastructure for live delivery — framing international expansion as an architecture-first challenge. **Paramount+** engineers Tom Schultz and Cesar Mascarenhas detailed how a single Amazon Fire TV codebase powers both Paramount+ and Pluto TV simultaneously, a dual-service consolidation model other streaming operators will likely study. **NBCUniversal** and **Comcast** contributed two separate sessions on AI-driven 4K Super Bowl upscaling and Peacock's evolving multiview product, supplying the day's most concrete case study in AI applied to premium live events.

On the show floor, **Sinclair** and ONE Media Technologies used the ATSC booth (Central Hall C1655) to demonstrate a single ATSC 3.0 transmission reaching TVs, tablets, and mobile devices simultaneously — a practical proof of the standard's device-agnostic delivery promise rather than another regulatory argument. Harvey Arnold, Sinclair's SVP of Engineering, received the 2026 NAB Television Engineering Achievement Award at Tuesday's We Are Broadcasters ceremony. In IP audio, **Calrec** debuted the Argo M, a standalone ST 2110 console in 24-, 36-, or 48-fader configurations that complements — and is distinct from — the ImPulse.V cloud software it announced Monday. **Lawo** is demonstrating the HOME Multiviewer, a server that monitors ST 2110, NDI, SRT, and JPEG XS signals natively in a single unit, sitting alongside its Edge ONE stagebox as a complementary monitoring layer.

In post and finishing, **Colorfront** launched Transkoder 2026 with a rebuilt Linux engine, AI-assisted QC, Dolby Vision 2 support, and Apple Vision Pro integration — a single-pipeline answer to facilities managing fragmented delivery specs. Sennheiser extended the Spectera platform with the SKM Handheld Transmitter, adding Neumann capsule compatibility and remote gain control for broadcast voice capture, with a September 2026 ship date.

Wednesday closes the show at 2pm PT. Watch for final-day wrap announcements on ATSC 3.0 infrastructure deals, any remaining streaming platform partnerships from the West Hall, and post-show data from NAB organizers on 2026 attendance versus last year's 54,000.
