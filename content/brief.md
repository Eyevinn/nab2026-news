---
title: "Streaming Summit opens with AI trust debate as floor vendors ship concrete tools"
date: "2026-04-20T15:30:00Z"
---

Day two of the exhibit hall and the opening day of the Streaming Summit produced a mix of concrete product releases and pointed industry framing. **Dan Rayburn** opened the Streaming Summit in the West Hall by declaring that AI trust — not AI capability — is "the most important currency" for streaming operators in 2026, shifting the conference conversation from what AI can do to whether audiences and industry partners will rely on it. The summit's first-day sessions included a Comcast and Peacock engineering deep dive on cloud-native multiview and Fox's Mayur Srinivasan presenting on the Fox One direct-to-consumer architecture.

On the floor, **TwelveLabs** made the most complete product pivot of the day, launching Pegasus 1.5 with automated Time-Based Metadata Extraction, a natural-language editing co-pilot called Rodeo, and an Autodesk Flow Capture integration — together repositioning the company from developer API to editorial workflow platform. **Ross Video** expanded the Carbonite HyperMax switcher with an additional row and flexible licensing and introduced the TouchDrive 5 control surface alongside a new NDI-native software switcher, Carbonite Code Solo. **Clear-Com** took home a NAB Best of Show award for the FreeSpeak Icon DECT beltpack while also launching a 4-channel HelixNet beltpack for complex multi-team live productions.

Storage was a recurring floor theme. **Studio Network Solutions** announced three products with stated prices — Outpost review-and-approval at $99/month, Trio S3-compatible cloud storage at $6/TB per month, and an on-premise AI Suite on perpetual license. **Promise Technology** introduced the Pegasus5 Thunderbolt 5 family including the R12 Pro with approximately 6,000 MB/s throughput and a new NVMe AI server line for 8K editing and inference workloads.

The day's most technically specific moment came from **Oracle** and five partners — Ateme, Broadpeak, Bitmovin, Wowza, and Cloudflare — running what appears to be the first public multi-vendor Media over QUIC (MoQ) interoperability demonstration at a major trade show.
