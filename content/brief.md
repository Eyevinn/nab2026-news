---
title: "NAB 2026 closes: AJA acquires Comprimato, 80+ Best of Show, Hall of Fame"
date: "2026-04-22T23:00:00Z"
---

NAB Show 2026 closed its exhibit hall at 2pm PT today, wrapping a four-day floor run that ended with a wave of formal recognition and strategic M&A. **AJA Video Systems** announced an agreement to acquire Comprimato, a Czech GPU-powered encoding software developer, unifying development behind AJA's BRIDGE LIVE IP product family. The deal signals a broader pattern visible across this year's show: hardware-first vendors acquiring software layers to deliver vertically integrated IP video workflows without relying on third-party partnerships.

**TV Technology** named more than 80 Best of Show winners spanning AI platforms, IP video, cloud playout, codecs, cameras, and storage — a list that included AJA BRIDGE LIVE IP, AWS Elemental Inference, Haivision Makito ONE, Harmonic Spectrum X Plus, Lawo Edge One, Signiant Connected Intelligence, and **Zixi** Market Switching. Zixi also positioned its ZEN Master platform at NAB as a direct satellite replacement, demonstrating dynamic market switching and SPTS re-multiplexing for broadcasters reassessing legacy delivery costs. **OWC** took the Future category award for its Express 4M2 Ultra, the first certified Thunderbolt 5 four-slot NVMe enclosure, which delivers up to 6,622 MB/s and starts at $399.99 when it ships in Q3 2026.

On the product side, **Canon** showed the MS-510 — a 1-inch SPAD sensor camera hitting 0.0006 lux minimum illumination, a 40% improvement over its predecessor, priced at $22,800 for sports and wildlife deployments. **Telos Alliance** introduced the Axia Pulsar AoIP console surface, Infinity VIP SE, and an Infinity IP Intercom MK2 with upgraded TFT displays, rounding out a show week that saw the company refresh both its hardware panels and cloud audio tooling in parallel.

The NAB Hall of Fame awards dinner on April 21 inducted Rob Lowe and John Tesh, with NAB President Curtis LeGeyt framing both as examples of the creative and entrepreneurial breadth of modern broadcasting. Post-show analyst notes — including Devoncroft's final Big Broadcast Survey data and Dan Rayburn's Streaming Summit attendance and session recaps — are expected to surface over the coming week.
