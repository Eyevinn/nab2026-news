---
title: "Hardware debuts, agentic AI deployments, and a stalled FCC clock dominate pre-show"
date: "2026-04-18T21:05:00Z"
---

NAB Show 2026's pre-show day has produced a wave of concrete hardware and software announcements ahead of the exhibit hall opening Sunday, April 19. On the production side, **EVS** is debuting T-Motion — a unified robotics platform spanning six camera systems built from its Telemetrics and XD Motion acquisitions — integrated directly with its live sports AI and asset management tools. **FOR-A**'s GoVertical! AiDi is already in commercial deployment at NBC Sports, converting 16:9 broadcast feeds to 9:16 in real time on NVIDIA RTX hardware with no cloud dependency. **GoPro** is making its professional cinema pitch at NAB with the MISSION 1 Series, a three-camera lineup built around a 50MP 1-inch sensor and the new GP3 5nm processor capable of 8K60.

On the captioning and workflow side, **AI-Media** is shipping its first new encoder hardware in more than a decade — the LEXI Text and LEXI Voice Encoders with 12G-SDI, upgraded AI processing, and a Hardware-as-Subscription model. **Dalet** has moved its Dalia agentic AI from early access to commercial availability, using natural-language orchestration to run full multi-step media workflows from a single user request, targeting both broadcast operations and marketing teams.

The agentic AI narrative is backed by customer numbers this cycle. A **Google Cloud** pre-show post names Cadent's 35% increase in campaign return on ad spend, Gray Media's AI-driven operation across 1,300 digital touchpoints, and MLB's real-time AI commentary as production-scale deployments — not pilots. Meanwhile, the ATSC 3.0 ecosystem is running up against a regulatory constraint: without an FCC-set ATSC 1.0 sunset date, supply-chain partners cannot commit to volume NextGen TV device production. Gray Media's Robert Folliard summarized the position bluntly: without a deadline, nothing happens.

The floor opens Sunday at 10 a.m. PT. Watch for whether hardware-defined stories — EVS robotics integration, FOR-A on-device AI, and Haivision's 5G transmitter — hold their technical claims under hands-on scrutiny, and whether the Streaming Summit's opening framing around AI trust (Dan Rayburn, April 20) sharpens or softens as vendor claims land on the show floor.
