title: "Day 1: AI agents, 100G cameras, and a new Sports Summit set the tone"
date: "2026-04-18T16:00:00Z"
---

Day one of NAB 2026 opens with a clear message from analysts and vendors alike: the proof-of-concept era is over. **Devoncroft Partners** and **Carlo De Marchis** are both telling exhibitors that generic "AI strategy" slides will fall flat this year — buyers want production-grade, governed, cost-modelled deployments. **Blue Lucy** is making that tangible with a talk on AI-governance workflows for broadcasters.

On the floor, the cameras stole the morning. **Blackmagic** shipped the URSA Cine Immersive 100G for Apple Immersive Video, **Sony** unveiled its R Series alongside the new XYN spatial-capture workflow and MOXELA, and **RED's** Cine-Broadcast Module is already running live on CBS and NBC Sports. **Avid** used the keynote slot to put Content Core on AWS — with Amazon MGM Studios as the launch customer — while **Harmonic** rolled out Spectrum X Plus with its own AI Orchestration layer, targeting cost-per-asset pressure in playout.

The two summits dominate the programme. The **Streaming Summit** (April 20–21) lines up leaders from Paramount, Prime Video, NBCUniversal, and JioStar — whose CTO will deep-dive on the 72-million-concurrent T20 World Cup stream. The brand-new four-day **Sports Summit**, anchored by NBC Sports' Jon Miller and powered by Lumen, opens the show floor to sports-rights, betting, and fan-engagement conversations that have never had this much stage time at NAB.

What to watch for Day 2: cloud-native post production, ATSC 3.0 updates, and the first vendor reactions to how loudly the "show us, don't tell us" message lands.
