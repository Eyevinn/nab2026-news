---
title: "NAB 2026 closes: final floor hardware, 72M concurrent streaming, and Best of Show"
date: "2026-04-22T16:10:00Z"
---

The exhibit hall at NAB 2026 closes at 2pm PT today, wrapping four days that covered hardware convergence, live sports streaming scale, and a consistent push toward IP-native production infrastructure. On the floor, **Riedel Communications** launched three ST 2110-native products including a HorizoN MultiViewer App that handles 128 picture-in-picture windows in a single 1RU device, while **Dejero** ran a live multi-booth demonstration of its TITAN Command device — a triple-5G laptop-sized unit linking five partner companies simultaneously across the show floor. Both products took TV Technology Best of Show awards on the final day.

**Signiant** previewed Connected Intelligence, an initiative that lets customers attach their own AI models to the Signiant Platform for prompt-triggered multi-step workflows, alongside a new Verify QC capability that validates content before it enters downstream pipelines. **Advanced HDR by Technicolor** and Cobalt Digital addressed the live sports mixed-format problem — where HDR camera feeds, SDR ad inserts, and graphics overlays coexist in the same signal chain — with frame-by-frame dynamic processing added to the Cobalt 9904 platform. Technicolor also confirmed ATSC 3.0 integration with RCA, ZapperBox, and three other converter box makers via Amlogic.

At the Streaming Summit, **Comcast** Principal Architect Derik Yarnell detailed the RealTime4K platform behind the Super Bowl LX delivery: 30 Mbps with Dolby Vision HDR and Dolby Atmos, built for live-event reliability constraints. A separate session examined how **JioHotStar** engineered CDN strategy, encoding pipelines, and capacity management for 72 million concurrent ICC T20 World Cup viewers — a benchmark significantly larger than the peak live sports figures typically cited by Western streaming operators.

**Shure's** DCA901 Planar Array Microphone, delivering eight spatially discrete audio channels over a single Dante cable from 78 integrated microphones, also won Best of Show on its first NAB appearance. ProVideo Coalition named **Lightcraft** Spark its Greatest Innovation pick and Adobe Color Mode in Premiere its Grand Prize. Post-show attendance figures and final Streaming Summit data releases from Dan Rayburn are expected this week.
