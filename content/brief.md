NAB Show 2026's pre-show conferences continue in Las Vegas ahead of the exhibit hall opening Sunday, April 19. The second wave of vendor announcements narrows around contribution hardware, sports streaming infrastructure, and a sharpening analyst consensus on what the industry actually needs from cloud deployments — not migration, but reliable, cost-efficient hybrid operation at scale.

**Haivision** is arriving at NAB with two distinct hardware launches. The Makito ONE places dual-channel H.264, HEVC, and JPEG XS encoding and decoding on a single hot-swappable blade, scaling to 36 channels in a 4RU chassis — a form-factor bet that multi-codec, multi-channel contribution hardware should consolidate rather than proliferate. The [Falkon X4](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/14/3273454/0/en/Haivision-Launches-Falkon-X4-Powering-a-New-Era-of-Ultra-Reliable-5G-Video-for-Live-Broadcast-and-Remote-Production.html) is a camera-mountable 5G transmitter bonding four MIMO modems, LEO satellite, and WiFi into a unit that also handles camera control, intercom, and local recording. Both products target live sports field production where kit consolidation and wireless reliability are operational priorities. **Lawo** adds to the hardware picture with the Edge One, a 1RU stagebox that converges 25 Gbps ST 2110 video and audio with swappable audio modules — aimed at outside broadcast and remote production venues.

On the streaming side, **Broadpeak's** Multiview builds multi-feed sports viewing at the packager layer rather than the encoder, sending one stream regardless of how many simultaneous feeds the viewer selects — a cost-efficiency argument backed by a Parks Associates finding that 53% of sports fans want the feature. **Wowza** and Cloudflare are demonstrating Media over QUIC live at NAB, running OBS output through CMAF over MoQT to sub-second last-mile delivery on a standards-based path. Analyst Dan Rayburn's [NETINT encoding survey analysis](https://www.streamingmediablog.com/2026/03/netint-encoding-survey.html) frames AV1 codec adoption as mainstream planning, with 40% of operators targeting 2026 deployment and combined reach projected at 57% by year-end.

The summit programme gains a notable addition: **FIFA** Head of Host Broadcast Production Oscar Sanchez will speak at the Sports Summit on April 19 — the first senior FIFA production executive on the NAB stage — weeks before the World Cup opens across the US, Canada, and Mexico. A NewscastStudio analysis captures the analytical temperature heading into the floor: broadcasters have stopped asking when to go fully cloud and are asking how to run hybrid reliably at flat budgets. What to watch Sunday: whether Haivision's blade-and-transmitter hardware story holds up against software-defined rivals, and how sports streaming operators respond to Broadpeak's packager-level multiview economics.
