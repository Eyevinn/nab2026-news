[Streaming Summit chairman Dan Rayburn kicked off the two-day summit](https://www.nabshow.com/las-vegas/conferences-and-workshops/streaming-summit/) on April 20 in the West Hall with a framing that positions AI credibility — not AI capability — as the central question for streaming operators. Rayburn told the opening session: "In the age of AI, trust will become the most important currency with consumers and within the industry." The argument is that as AI tools proliferate across production and delivery pipelines, the ability of platforms to demonstrate that AI-generated or AI-assisted content is reliable and transparent becomes as important as the technical capability itself.

The summit runs through April 21 with approximately 85 speakers across two concurrent tracks. Sessions on opening day include Comcast's cloud architecture for Peacock multiview sports, Fox's Mayur Srinivasan presenting on the Fox One direct-to-consumer platform, and an engineering case study on JioHotStar's delivery infrastructure for live sports at peak concurrent viewer scale. Rayburn will return Tuesday to present current facts and figures on the sports streaming market, including live-event viewership breakdowns.

The shift in framing is notable. Previous Streaming Summit editions have centered on "AI is coming" positioning; the 2026 opening framing assumes AI is already deployed and moves the conversation to accountability, governance, and consumer confidence. For streaming operators integrating AI into recommendation engines, content moderation, and automated production, the trust question has practical compliance and product design implications that go beyond technical performance metrics.
