[Giles Baker, Dolby's SVP of Cloud Solutions, led the "Fan Experience Reimagined" panel at NAB's Sports Summit on April 21](https://www.tvtechnology.com/events/sports-summit-goes-into-overtime), introducing new capabilities in Dolby OptiView — the company's live sports streaming platform. The additions include an Ad Engine designed for smoother live ad playback, hybrid dynamic ad insertion combining server-side and guided ad insertion (SSAI/SGAI), and a Sports Intelligence layer that surfaces personalized fan moments during live events.

IBM and AWS participated in the panel alongside Dolby, with the session framing personalization — not just picture quality — as the central battleground for sports streaming platforms. Dolby's Paul Boustead had presented a related session Monday on building personalized fan experiences, giving the OptiView roadmap two sessions of visibility across the two-day Sports Summit program.

The ad insertion capabilities are notable given the ongoing shift of live sports rights toward streaming platforms, where ad-supported models need to perform under the same latency and reliability constraints as linear broadcast. Hybrid SSAI/SGAI approaches attempt to reduce the buffering and slate-duration problems that have historically made live ad insertion a weaker experience on streaming than on broadcast.
