The [2026 NAB Streaming Summit](http://nabstreamingsummit.com/) (April 20–21) devotes two sessions specifically to live sports streaming: "The Complex and Fragmented World of the Sports Streaming Business" and a companion session, "Defining The User Experience for Live Sports Streaming." Together they put rights-map complexity and UX design at the center of the streaming operations agenda, separate from the Summit's general monetization and churn tracks.

The speaker mix is notable for pairing traditional rights holders — NASCAR and PGA TOUR — with newer entrants including DAZN, FanDuel, Amazon Prime Video, and Warner Bros. Discovery. That combination reflects the practical reality: live sports content in 2026 is distributed simultaneously across broadcast, cable, SVOD, FAST, and sportsbook platforms, making rights mapping and consistent user experience more operationally demanding than for scripted or library content.

Together with the NAB Sports Summit's parallel four-day programming in the West Hall Sports Theater, NAB 2026 features more sports-focused conference content across both tracks than any prior show year. The dual-summit structure treats sports streaming not as a specialty topic but as a core operational and business-model challenge for anyone running a video platform at scale.
