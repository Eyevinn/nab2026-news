- **Summary**: Streaming Summit chair Dan Rayburn opened the April 20 session with a pointed critique of AI-generated misinformation, arguing that trust — not technology capability — is now the primary currency in the streaming business. Rayburn cited a ChatGPT-generated fabrication that attributed false quotes to him about Peacock's Super Bowl stream performance, including invented rebuffering and authentication delay claims. He estimated that 75% of industry content shared on LinkedIn contains inaccurate information, citing a post that misrepresented Meta's $201 billion gross revenue as profit and received 47 shares before corrections. Google Gemini reportedly recommended an app that had been shut down for 12 years. Rayburn acknowledged legitimate AI use — the PGA Tour's AI clip system generates 7,000 daily clips — but concluded the industry must prioritize human accountability over automated output.
- **Quote**: "Trust has been eroded."
